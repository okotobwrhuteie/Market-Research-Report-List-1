<p><p>High-speed analog-to-digital converters (ADCs) are classified into two main types: High Speed and Ultra High Speed. High Speed ADCs typically have sampling rates ranging from a few hundred megahertz to a few gigahertz, making them suitable for applications requiring fast analog signal processing. On the other hand, Ultra High Speed ADCs have even higher sampling rates, often exceeding tens of gigahertz, and are used in demanding applications such as radar systems and high-frequency communication systems that require extremely fast signal conversion with high precision.</p></p>

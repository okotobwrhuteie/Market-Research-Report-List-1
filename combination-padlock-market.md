<p><p>Combination padlocks come in two main types: multiple-dial locks and single-dial locks. Multiple-dial locks require users to turn multiple dials to align the correct combination, typically offering more possible combinations and higher security. Single-dial locks, on the other hand, only have one dial to turn to set the combination, making them easier to use but potentially less secure. Both types are popular in the market, with multiple-dial locks being preferred for higher security needs and single-dial locks for simpler applications.</p></p>

<p><ul><li>Hand Layup</li><li>Resin Transfer Molding</li><li>Vacuum Infusion Processing</li><li>Injection Molding</li><li>Compression Molding</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Automotive carbon fiber composites are used in various manufacturing processes such as hand layup, resin transfer molding, vacuum infusion processing, injection molding, and compression molding. Hand layup involves manually layering carbon fiber sheets and resin. Resin transfer molding injects resin into a closed mold with reinforcement material. Vacuum infusion processing uses a vacuum to distribute resin through the reinforcement material. Injection molding injects molten material into a mold cavity. Compression molding involves pressing material into a heated mold. Each process offers unique benefits for producing automotive carbon fiber composites.</p></p>

<p><p>Space debris monitoring and removal refers to the process of tracking and eliminating space debris or space junk in Earth's orbit. Space debris primarily consists of defunct satellites, spent rocket stages, and other fragments generated from previous space missions. Space debris poses a significant threat to operational satellites and spacecraft, as even small pieces can cause catastrophic damage through high-velocity collisions.</p><p>The market for space debris monitoring and removal is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. The increasing number of satellites being launched into space, along with the rising awareness about the potential risks posed by space debris, are the key driving factors for market growth. Governments and space agencies are actively investing in developing technologies and systems to monitor and remove space debris.</p><p>Some of the latest trends in the space debris monitoring and removal market include the development of advanced tracking systems, such as radar and optical sensors, to accurately monitor and predict the movement of space debris. Additionally, there is a growing focus on the development of innovative debris removal technologies, such as active debris removal (ADR) systems and space tugs. These technologies aim to capture and remove space debris from orbit, either by deorbiting them or relocating them to safer orbits.</p><p>The market growth analysis suggests that the space debris monitoring and removal market is expected to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 8.3% during the forecast period.

<p><p>Antimicrobial coatings for medical devices come in two main types: metallic coatings and non-metallic coatings. Metallic coatings, such as silver and copper coatings, utilize the antimicrobial properties of metals to inhibit the growth of bacteria and other pathogens on the surface of medical devices. Non-metallic coatings, including polymer coatings and organic coatings, work by forming a protective barrier that prevents the colonization of microbes. Both types of coatings play a crucial role in reducing the risk of infections in healthcare settings.</p></p>

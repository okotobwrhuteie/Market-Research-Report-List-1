<p><ul><li>Hall Effect</li><li>Fluxgate</li><li>Magneto Resistive</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The 3-axis electronic compass market consists of three types: Hall Effect, Fluxgate, and Magneto Resistive. The Hall Effect compass uses a sensor that relies on the Hall Effect phenomenon to measure the magnetic field, allowing for accurate readings. Fluxgate compasses, on the other hand, employ a coil to detect the magnetic field by measuring voltage changes. Lastly, Magneto Resistive compasses use a sensor that measures the changes in resistance of a material when exposed to a magnetic field, providing precise direction information.</p></p>

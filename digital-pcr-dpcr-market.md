<p><ul><li>Microfluidic dPCR</li><li>Droplet Digital PCR (ddPCR)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Digital PCR (dPCR) is a technology that divides a DNA sample into thousands of individual reactions and amplifies them independently to achieve higher sensitivity and accuracy in quantifying nucleic acid targets. There are two main types of dPCR technologies in the market. The first is microfluidic dPCR, which uses microfluidic chips to compartmentalize the sample. The second is Droplet Digital PCR (ddPCR), which creates thousands of tiny water-in-oil droplets containing the sample for amplification. Both technologies offer precise and reliable results for various applications in research and diagnostics.</p></p>

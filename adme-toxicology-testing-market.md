<p><p>ADME-Toxicology testing refers to a set of tests conducted to assess the absorption, distribution, metabolism, excretion, and toxicity of drugs and other substances. In-vitro testing involves performing these tests outside of a living organism, using techniques such as microsomes or cell cultures. Biochemical testing analyzes the metabolic components in drug samples. Cell testing utilizes isolated cells or tissues to study their response to substances. In-vivo testing involves studying the effects of substances within a living organism. These testing methods provide crucial information on the safety and efficacy of drugs and chemicals.</p></p>

<p><p>Feed carbohydrate market types include glucose, sucrose, starch, and cellulose. Glucose is a simple sugar that provides a quick source of energy. Sucrose is a combination of glucose and fructose, commonly found in sugar cane and sugar beets. Starch is a complex carbohydrate found in grains and root vegetables, providing a sustained release of energy. Cellulose is a structural carbohydrate found in plant cell walls, mainly used for fiber in animal diets. Each type of carbohydrate serves a specific purpose in the feed industry.</p></p>

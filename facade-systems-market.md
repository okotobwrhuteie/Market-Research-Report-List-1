<p><p>Facade systems refer to the outer layer of a building that provides protection from weather conditions and enhances aesthetic appeal. Ventilated facade systems allow for airflow between the facade and the building structure, while non-ventilated facade systems do not. Ventilated systems are ideal for improving energy efficiency and preventing moisture buildup, while non-ventilated systems are simpler and more cost-effective. Both types have their own advantages and are chosen based on the specific requirements of the building project.</p></p>

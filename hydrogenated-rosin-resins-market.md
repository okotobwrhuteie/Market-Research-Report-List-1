<p><p>Hydrogenated Rosin Resins are categorized into two main types based on the level of hydrogenation: Partially Hydrogenated Rosin Resin and Highly Hydrogenated Rosin Resin. Partially Hydrogenated Rosin Resin has a lower degree of hydrogenation, resulting in a softer and more flexible resin, ideal for applications such as adhesives and coatings. On the other hand, Highly Hydrogenated Rosin Resin undergoes a more extensive hydrogenation process, leading to a harder and more thermally stable resin that is commonly used in high-performance adhesives and electronics.</p></p>

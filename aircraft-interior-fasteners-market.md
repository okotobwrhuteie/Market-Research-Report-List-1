<p><p>The Aircraft Interior Fasteners Market consists of two main types: Threaded Fasteners and Non-Threaded Fasteners. Threaded Fasteners are designed with ridges (threads) on their surface, allowing them to securely attach two or more components together. Common examples include screws and bolts. On the other hand, Non-Threaded Fasteners do not have threads but rely on other mechanisms, such as pins, clips, or rivets, for attachment. Both types play crucial roles in the aircraft interior, ensuring structural integrity, safety, and ease of maintenance.</p></p>

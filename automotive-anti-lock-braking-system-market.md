<p><p>Automotive Anti-Lock Braking Systems are essential safety features in vehicles designed to prevent wheels from locking up during braking, thereby maintaining steering control. Two main types exist: Pneumatic Anti-Lock Braking Systems utilize pressurized air to regulate braking pressure, while Hydraulic Anti-Lock Braking Systems use hydraulic fluid for the same purpose. Both systems function by automatically modulating braking pressure to prevent skidding and improve overall safety on the road. The choice between these systems depends on the specific needs and requirements of the vehicle.</p></p>

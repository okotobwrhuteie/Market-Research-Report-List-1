<p><p>Hydrogen fuel cell vehicles are mainly categorized into three types of fuel cell technologies: Proton Exchange Membrane Fuel Cells (PEMFCs), Phosphoric Acid Fuel Cells (PAFCs), and others. PEMFCs are commonly used in commercial vehicles due to their high power density and efficiency. PAFCs are typically used in stationary applications like buses and backup power systems. Other types of fuel cells include Solid Oxide Fuel Cells (SOFCs) and Molten Carbonate Fuel Cells (MCFCs), which are still in development but show promise for various transportation and energy applications.</p></p>

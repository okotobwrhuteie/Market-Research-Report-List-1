<p><p>Thin-film semiconductor deposition is a crucial process in the production of electronic devices. Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) involves depositing a thin film of material on a substrate using chemical reactions. Physical Vapor Deposition (PVD) involves depositing a thin film by evaporating and condensing a material onto a substrate. Both processes are widely used in industries such as electronics, solar cells, and optical coatings. Each method has its advantages and applications depending on the specific requirements of the thin film being deposited.</p></p>

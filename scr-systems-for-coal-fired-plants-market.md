<p><p>SCR systems, or Selective Catalytic Reduction systems, are used in coal-fired plants to reduce nitrogen oxide (NOx) emissions. There are three main types of SCR systems: Anhydrous Ammonia, Aqueous Ammonia, and Ureas. Anhydrous Ammonia SCR systems use gaseous ammonia, while Aqueous Ammonia SCR systems use a solution of ammonia in water. Ureas SCR systems use a solid material called urea, which is converted into ammonia within the system. These SCR systems help coal-fired plants comply with emission regulations by converting NOx into less harmful substances.</p></p>
